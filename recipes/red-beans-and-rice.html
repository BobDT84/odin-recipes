Red Beans And Rice

Cookware

  • Crockpot
  • Large Spoon
  • Knife
  • Cutting Board
  • Meidum Pot or a Rice cooker
  • (Optional) Pan

Ingredients

  • 2 cans of kidney beans
  • 1 smoked sausage
    • 1 bell pepper
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 or 4 ribs of celery
  • Liquid shrimp & crab boil
  • Small bag of rice
  • (Optional) 3 bay leaves

Instructions

Use the knife and cutting board to dice your bell pepper, onion, and celery. If you want to reduce the water in the crockpot, then use a pan on the stove at medium heat to sweat the vegetables before pouring them into the crock pot. Drain the liquid from the cans of kidney beans and pour them into the crockpot. Stir the beans and the vegetables until evenly distributed, and turn the crockpot on high. Slice your smoked sausage into round disks before adding them to the crockpot. Pour in 1 or 2 half cap full of the liquid shrimp & crab boil. Finally, add the 3 bay leaves, if you have them, and lock the lid on the crockpot and let the ingredients cook for 4 hours on high. Give a stir after 2 hours.

With 1 hour remaining of the previous ingredients cooking in the crockpot, use Google to search for instructions on how to cook rice. Cooking rice has been, and continues to be one of my weaknesses.

Once the rice and beans are cooked, open the lid of the crockpot and enjoy the wonderful smells and steam bath that will instantly fog your glasses. If you can easily find the bay leaves, then go ahead and remove them now. I prefer to add rice to a bowl, and stir in just the liquids from the crockpot before adding the beans, vegetables, and sausage. I aim for a ratio of 2/3 rice to 1/3 other. But the great thing about red beans and rice is that it is affordable, and you can experiment with multiple styles of plating.
